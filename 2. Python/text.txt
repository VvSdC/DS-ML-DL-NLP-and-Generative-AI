Sachin Ramesh Tendulkar, born on April 24, 1973, in Bombay (now Mumbai), India, is widely regarded as one of the greatest cricketers in the history of the sport. 

Nicknamed the Little Master and Master Blaster, Tendulkar’s career spanned over two decades, during which he became a symbol of excellence and consistency in world cricket. Making his international debut in 1989 at the age of 16 against Pakistan, he quickly established himself as a prodigious talent with his impeccable technique and fearless stroke play. Over the years, he amassed a record-breaking 100 international centuries, became the first player to score a double century in One Day Internationals, and remains the highest run-scorer in both Test and ODI formats.

Tendulkar’s contribution went beyond statistics—he carried the hopes of millions of fans, often single-handedly anchoring India’s batting lineup during crucial matches. His illustrious career culminated in India’s 2011 World Cup victory, a moment he described as the proudest day of his life. Off the field, Tendulkar is admired for his humility, discipline, and dedication, which earned him the Bharat Ratna, India’s highest civilian award, in 2014. 

Even after retirement in 2013, he continues to inspire generations as a mentor, philanthropist, and ambassador of the game, embodying the spirit of cricket both in India and globally.
